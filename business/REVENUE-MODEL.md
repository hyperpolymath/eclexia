# Eclexia Revenue Model

## Principles
- Keep the language and core runtime open.
- Monetize deployment velocity, reliability guarantees, and organizational enablement.
- Align pricing with measurable economic and sustainability outcomes.

## Revenue Streams
1. Enterprise support subscriptions
- SLA-backed support
- Security advisories and patch response windows
- Architecture and migration guidance

2. Hosted platform services
- Managed execution insights
- Policy dashboards for budgets and carbon constraints
- Team/organization reporting and governance controls

3. Professional services
- Pilot implementation and integration
- Domain-specific optimization engagements
- Runtime and policy tuning

4. Training and certification
- Developer onboarding curricula
- Platform engineer certification
- Internal enablement programs for enterprise customers

5. Partnership programs
- Joint projects with academia and industry
- Co-funded applied research and case studies

## Packaging Sketch
- Community: open source language/runtime, self-hosted tooling.
- Team: managed dashboards, advanced reports, collaboration features.
- Enterprise: SSO, governance controls, premium support, deployment assistance.

## Pricing Considerations
- Per-seat for training and team collaboration features.
- Usage-based components for hosted analytics.
- Fixed-fee or milestone-based professional services.

## Near-Term Priority
1. Support + services for early adopters.
2. Pilot-backed case studies proving value.
3. Introduce hosted capabilities once usage patterns stabilize.

## Success Metrics
- Time to production pilot.
- Reduction in budget-violation incidents.
- Reduction in resource spend and/or carbon intensity.
- Renewal and expansion rates for support customers.
